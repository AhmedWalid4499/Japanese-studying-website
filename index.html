あ Hiragana & Katakana

Kana are the two Japanese syllabic alphabets. Hiragana (ひらがな) is used for native Japanese words and grammar. Katakana (カタカナ) is used for foreign words, loan words, and emphasis. You must master both before the N5 exam! 🌸

🔄 Adjective Opposites
い-adjectives い

End in い. To make negative: remove い → add くない. Past: remove い → add かった.

Example: たかい → たかくない (not expensive) → たかかった (was expensive)

な-adjectives な

Add な before a noun. Negative: じゃないです. Past: replace です → でした.

Example: しずかな へや → しずかじゃないです (not quiet)

🔢 Japanese Counters (助数詞)

In Japanese, you cannot just say "one book" or "three cats" — you must attach a counter suffix (助数詞 じょすうし) to the number. The counter changes depending on what you're counting. Many counters also have irregular readings at 1, 3, 6, 8, and 10 — these are highlighted in irregular badges below. 🌸
